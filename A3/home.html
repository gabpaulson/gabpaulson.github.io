Gab in Gabon

The summer of 2019 was spent in Gabon with my friend Maddie, and many others!

Food

Africa, particularly Central Africa is abundant in fresh fruit, but for meals, dishes are often carb- and meat-heavy. Fresh fish and chicken are commonly seen, in the wild and on your plate. Sometimes you get to try "bush meat" which is anything that is hunted and sold - monkey, toucan-like birds, pangolins, cat, crocodile, sea turtle... I was offered boa and antelope.

Manioc

"Manioc" or cassava root, made into flour, fermented, wrapped in leaves, and boiled, has a flavored reminiscent of sourdough bread. It's served with most meals, often paried with a sauce or cassava leaf stew, "feuilles de manioc".

Piment

The peppers of Gabon are beloved and added to many meals, usually whole peppers added while a dish is cooking, or blended into a hot sauce.

Hot hands!

Don't handle these peppers with your bare hands, especially while chopping. Our friend did this, and his hands burned excruciatingly for 5 hours.

Friends

Our friends, the Otoungas, hosted us for three weeks, teaching us about the city of Makokou, connecting us with a coiffeuse, feeding us good food, and throwing dance parties. We miss them! Good thing for WhatsApp.

Fun

We had much fun, during and between all the volunteering we did. One day we took a pirogue, or narrow, dugout canoe across the river.
